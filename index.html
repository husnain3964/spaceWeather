space weather

Space weather encompasses the effects of solar and cosmic activity, including solar wind, solar flares, and geomagnetic storms, on the Earth's magnetosphere, atmosphere, and technological systems.

Solar wind

Solar wind is a flow of charged particles, mostly protons and electrons, that continuously streams out from the Sun’s outer atmosphere, called the corona

geomagnetic storm

A geomagnetic storm is a disturbance in Earth's magnetic field caused by interactions with intense solar wind, often resulting in auroras and potential impacts on technology.

Solar flares

Solar flares are sudden, intense bursts of radiation and energy on the Sun’s surface, caused by the release of magnetic energy.

solar radiation storm

is a high-energy event caused by a burst of solar energetic particles (mostly protons) from the Sun, which can impact space weather, satellite operations, and human health in space.

Coronal Mass Ejections

(CMEs) are large expulsions of plasma and magnetic field from the Sun's corona into space, which can impact space weather and Earth's magnetosphere

sunspot

A sunspot is a temporary, dark spot on the Sun's surface caused by intense magnetic activity that inhibits convection and reduces the local temperature.

Space-time

is the four-dimensional continuum that combines the three dimensions of space with the one dimension of time, forming a unified framework in which all physical events occur and are measured.

Sun's magnetic field

is a dynamic magnetic field generated by the movement of charged particles within the Sun, affecting solar activity and extending throughout the solar system.
